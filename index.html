Guide

Tips for a word cloud that's worth sharing

A few small choices separate a cluttered cloud from one people actually want to look at.

”

Filter stopwords first

Leave stopword filtering on so "the," "and," and "to" don't dominate words that actually matter.

”

Match the shape to the message

A heart for a love letter, a star for an achievement, a cloud for a brainstorm — the outline can carry meaning too.

”

Keep your palette to one mood

Sunset and candy read playful; ink and monochrome read serious. Pick the palette that matches your context, not just your favorite color.

”

Widen the size range for fewer words

If you only have ten or so words, push the max font size up and the min down — it fills the shape more confidently.

”

Use manual mode for branded terms

If a product name should dominate regardless of how often it's mentioned, manual weighting is the more reliable choice.

”

Export SVG for print

SVG stays crisp at any size, which matters if you're printing a poster or putting the cloud on a large canvas.

Glossary

Word cloud terms, in plain language

A quick reference if any of the controls feel unfamiliar.

§

Word weight

A number that decides how large a word appears. Higher weight, bigger word.

§

Stopwords

Common connective words like "the," "is," and "of" that carry little meaning on their own.

§

Shape mask

An outline — like a heart or star — that constrains where words are allowed to be placed.

§

Spiral placement

The layout method that positions each word by walking outward from the center until it finds open space.
